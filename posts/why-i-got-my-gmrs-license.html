Why I Got My GMRS License

10 November 20

My first base station. A Kenwood TK-880-1.

If you have never heard of GMRS (General Mobile Radio Service) before, no worries, most people haven't. Up until this year, I wasn't familiar with it either. I somewhat stumbled upon this obscured technology through listening to an episode of Ask Noah Show a few years back.

I don't remember what the episode was about, only that Noah was saying something about "ham" radio. He later went on to talk about the perks of being an amateur radio operator and a particular situation where it helped him get out of a pickle. By the end of the episode, I think I already had a BaoFeng UV-5R radio in my Amazon cart and a new hobby in the making.

Fast forward a couple of years later, and still now license. My ambitious attempt to become an amateur radio operator was short-lived and put on the back burner. My radio was now mostly a geeky decoration and the occasional flashlight when a storm would take out the electricity.

Now comes the year 2020, the "Karen" of all years. And, right before the pandemic (COVID-19) started to catch fire, my wife and I bought our first home in a small HOA neighborhood in La Porte, TX. If you have never heard or been to this area, it's adjacent to the bay and partially surrounded by refineries and chemical plants.
